T2DM Equity-Efficiency Decision Aid Tool

Evaluate how changing risk, efficacy, and monthly costs shape preferences for T2DM treatments under different equity-efficiency scenarios (self-focused, equal outcomes, or unequal outcomes).

Introduction

This tool considers three experiments:

  1. Experiment 1 (Self-Focused): only personal risk, efficacy, and cost.
  2. Experiment 2 (Equal Outcomes): health outcomes shared equally with others who have poorer health.
  3. Experiment 3 (Unequal Outcomes): separate risk, efficacy, and cost parameters for “others,” highlighting equity trade-offs.

Adjust side-effect risk (%), efficacy (% chance of reaching target A1C), and monthly out-of-pocket cost ($0–1000) to see how preferences shift. The tool computes predicted plan uptake, willingness to pay for each attribute, scenario saving, and cost-benefit analysis (via QALYs).

Instructions

  1. Attributes: Review risk (0% baseline, up to 30%), efficacy (10% baseline, 50%/90%), and monthly cost (0–1000 USD).
  2. Inputs: Select an experiment. If Exp3, fill in “others” attributes too. Click “Predict Health Plan Uptake.”
  3. Predicted Uptake: Shows a radial gauge with probability of choosing the plan vs. opting out.
  4. WTP Results: Click “Show WTP Plots” for bar charts of how participants value changes in risk or efficacy.
  5. Scenarios: Save configurations, compare average WTP for risk across experiments, or export scenarios to PDF.
  6. Costs & Benefits: Overviews fixed and variable cost items with QALY-based benefits, net benefit, and relevant charts.

Key Attributes

Efficacy

Chance of reaching target A1C in 6 months. Baseline 10%, or 50%/90% in scenarios.

Efficacy

Higher efficacy → better T2DM control outcomes.

Risk

Probability of side effects from 0% baseline up to 30%.

Risk

Greater side-effect risk implies higher compensation needed to accept it.

Monthly Cost

Ranges from $0 to $1000. Higher costs reduce the likelihood of plan uptake.

Monthly Out-of-Pocket Cost

Direct monthly expense for T2DM treatments or coverage.

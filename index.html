T2DM Equity-Efficiency Decision Aid Tool

Evaluate how changing risk, efficacy, and monthly costs affect preferences for T2DM treatments under different equity-efficiency scenarios.

Introduction

This tool includes three experiments for T2DM plan preferences:

  1. Experiment 1: Self-focused (only personal cost, risk, efficacy).
  2. Experiment 2: Equal outcomes with others.
  3. Experiment 3: Unequal outcomes with separate “others” attributes.

By adjusting side-effect risk, efficacy, and monthly cost ($0–$1000), you can predict plan uptake, explore willingness to pay for each attribute, and conduct cost-benefit (QALY-based) analysis.

Instructions

  1. Attributes Tab: Learn about risk, efficacy, cost (0–1000). Reference levels: Risk=0%, Efficacy=10%.
  2. Inputs Tab: Select experiment (Exp3 includes “others”), set attribute levels, click “Predict Health Plan Uptake.”
  3. Predicted Health Plan Uptake Tab: Shows radial gauge of adoption probability vs. opt-out.
  4. WTP Results Tab: Generate bar charts for risk/efficacy WTP (negative = disutility, positive = willingness to pay).
  5. Scenarios Tab: Save scenario configurations, compare average WTP for risk across experiments, or export as PDF.
  6. Costs & Benefits Tab: Insurance-like cost items plus QALY-based benefits and net benefit calculations.

Key Attributes

Efficacy

Chance of reaching target A1C in 6 months (10% baseline, or 50%/90% as alternatives).

Efficacy

Higher efficacy → stronger T2DM control outcomes.

Risk

Side-effect probability: 0% baseline, or 8%, 16%, 30% in scenarios.

Risk

Higher risk requires more compensation to accept potential side effects.

Monthly Cost

Ranges from $0 to $1000. Higher cost lowers plan adoption likelihood.

Monthly Out-of-Pocket Cost

Direct monthly expense for T2DM treatments or coverage.
